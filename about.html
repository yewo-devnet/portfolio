About Me

My name is Yewo Mkandawire, a passionate and driven third-year Computer Network Engineering student at the University of Malawi (UNIMA)—the country's top institution. With a strong foundation in programming, networking, and system administration, I am building a career at the intersection of technology, entrepreneurship, agriculture, and leadership.

Over the past three years, my academic journey has equipped me with essential skills in web development, database systems, network security, cloud computing, and artificial intelligence. I've completed a rigorous curriculum (as seen in my modules), complemented by independent learning through programs like CS50x and CS50P, which continue to sharpen my problem-solving and systems thinking capabilities.

But learning goes beyond the classroom. My passion for electronics and technology began early—crafting simple machines like hand fans with motors, clay, and wires. Today, that same curiosity drives me to explore systems programming, cybersecurity, and intelligent networks that can transform lives and communities.

Entrepreneurial Experience

My entrepreneurial spirit has always been active. From helping my grandmother with her grocery stall and reselling second-hand phones, to farming and refurbishing laptops, I've consistently found creative ways to solve problems and build value. At university, I founded a small tech reselling operation and co-managed a one-hectare maize farm, merging business sense with practical experience.

These ventures have taught me not just financial literacy and marketing, but resilience, teamwork, and leadership—qualities I bring into every collaboration.
